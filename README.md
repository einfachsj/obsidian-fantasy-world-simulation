# A Fantasy World Simulation for Obsidian
Obsidian Fantasy World Simulation turns your Obsidian graph into a living world. Nodes act as inhabitants, notes become places they interact with. Simulate economies, settlements, dungeons, and evolving lore—transforming your vault into a dynamic, interconnected reality. With cycles and an LLM, the world grows daily!

---
Fantasy World Simulation for Obsidian is an experimental project that transforms a note-taking environment into a living, autonomous fantasy world. The core idea is simple: simulate a world. The execution, however, is complex.

Obsidian acts as the visual game surface, where folders, notes, and the graph view represent the structure of the world. Notes are not just text—they become buildings, locations, characters, and systems. The player is not a traditional player, but an observer and indirect creator, able to influence the world by defining structures, rules, and content.

At the heart of the simulation is an LLM-driven system. It generates and updates notes using structured frontmatter, allowing entities such as inhabitants to evolve over time. Each resident is treated as an agent with emerging personality traits, behaviors, and life cycles. Their decisions, growth, and interactions are influenced by both predefined logic and AI-driven interpretation.

The world itself is a medieval fantasy setting inspired by systems like Dungeons & Dragons and isekai narratives. A unique core mechanic is the day–night cycle: during the day, inhabitants build, trade, explore, and develop settlements; during the night, they are forcibly transported into dangerous dungeon instances. These dungeon phases act as high-risk events that can reduce the population and reshape the world dynamically.

A central narrative element is the planet Amnesia, created by a robotic entity named Atom. This AI acts as both creator and overseer, generating the first inhabitants and guiding early civilization. Over time, a deeper conflict emerges through the existence of the 25th individual, whose actions introduce a persistent curse tied to a black hole and the dungeon system.

Current Features
Resident interaction and management system
Day–night cycle with automated transitions
Nightly dungeon event system
AI-generated inhabitants and settlements
Manual creation of custom residents
Functional planet generation system
Work in Progress
Item database and crafting system
Economy balancing and trade mechanics
Expanded AI personality and decision systems
Interactive world map integration
Vision

The long-term goal is to create a self-evolving simulation where knowledge, structure, and AI merge into a persistent world. Every note becomes part of a larger system, every connection a possible interaction. The vault is no longer static—it becomes a living, breathing ecosystem shaped by time, logic, and imagination.
